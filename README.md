# Sales Data Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes retail sales data to evaluate product performance, category performance, sales volume, and monthly revenue trends. The goal is to provide actionable business insights that can help management make informed decisions regarding marketing, inventory, and revenue growth.

The analysis was conducted using Python, Pandas, NumPy, and Matplotlib.

---

## Business Objectives

The company seeks to answer the following questions:

1. Which product generates the highest revenue?
2. Which product sells the highest quantity?
3. Which category performs best?
4. What are the monthly revenue trends?
5. Which month recorded the highest and lowest revenue?
6. Is revenue increasing over time?
7. Which products should receive additional marketing investment?

---

## Dataset Description

The dataset contains sales transaction records with the following fields:

| Column | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Date | Date of transaction |
| Product | Product sold |
| Category | Product category |
| Quantity | Number of units sold |
| Unit_Price | Selling price per unit |

## KPIs Created

### Revenue

Revenue = Quantity × Unit Price

### Month

Extracted from the transaction date to support monthly trend analysis.

### Revenue Growth Rate

Calculated using percentage change to evaluate month-over-month revenue performance.

## Key Findings

- Phone generated the highest total revenue.
- Chair recorded one of the highest sales volumes but generated comparatively lower revenue due to its lower unit price.
- Electronics was the highest-performing category.
- Revenue fluctuated significantly across months rather than increasing consistently.
- March recorded the highest month-over-month revenue growth rate.
- May generated the highest total revenue.
- A weak negative correlation was observed between Quantity Sold and Revenue, indicating that higher sales volume did not always translate into higher revenue due to differences in product pricing.

---

## Business Insights

- Revenue performance is influenced by both sales volume and unit price.
- High-volume products may contribute less revenue when sold at lower prices.
- Premium-priced products can generate strong revenue despite lower sales volume.
- Monthly revenue volatility suggests the need for improved sales forecasting and planning.

---

## Recommendations

1. Increase marketing investment in high-revenue products such as Phones.
2. Investigate factors responsible for revenue fluctuations across months.
3. Review pricing and promotional strategies for high-volume, low-revenue products.
4. Focus inventory planning on consistently high-performing products.
5. Implement monthly revenue monitoring to identify emerging sales trends early.
